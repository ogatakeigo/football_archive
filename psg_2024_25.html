Paris Saint-Germain's 2024-25 season was a historic landmark. In their first campaign since 2016-17 without all-time top scorer Kylian Mbappé, manager Luis Enrique orchestrated an absolute masterclass, building a collective, dynamic, and fluid team. The additions of João Neves, Willian Pacho, and Désiré Doué perfectly complemented a young, hungry core. PSG dominated Ligue 1, securing their 13th league title with 84 points, 92 goals scored, and a breathtaking brand of possession football. Ousmane Dembélé emerged as the absolute talisman, winning the Golden Boot with 21 league goals, while Bradley Barcola provided a league-high 10 assists.

But it was in cup competitions where PSG truly cemented their legacy. They claimed the Coupe de France and the Trophée des Champions domestically, but the crowning achievement was their triumph in the UEFA Champions League. Driven by a stellar midfield trio of Vitinha, João Neves, and Warren Zaïre-Emery, and an impenetrable defense anchored by Marquinhos, PSG vanquished their European demons. They delivered an astonishing 5-0 victory over Inter Milan in the final, achieving the first-ever continental treble in French football history. The 2024-25 season will forever be remembered as the year PSG finally conquered Europe while maintaining absolute domestic supremacy.

03 / TACTICS

Tactics

PSG under Luis Enrique evolved into a relentless, possession-based 4-3-3 machine. Their game was fundamentally built on extreme ball control, high-intensity counter-pressing, and fluid positional rotations. From the back, Gianluigi Donnarumma and the center-back pairing of Marquinhos and Willian Pacho acted as the primary distributors, effortlessly bypassing opposing presses. The midfield was the true engine of the team; Vitinha operated as the deep-lying playmaker alongside the tireless João Neves and Warren Zaïre-Emery, forming a trio that suffocated opponents through sheer technical superiority and unmatched stamina.

In attack, PSG showcased a highly adaptable and unpredictable structure. Unlike previous seasons that relied heavily on individual superstars, the 2024-25 side thrived on collective movement. Ousmane Dembélé operated as an inverted playmaker from the right, drifting inside to devastating effect, while Bradley Barcola hugged the left touchline to isolate defenders in one-on-one situations. Through the middle, Marco Asensio or Kang-in Lee frequently dropped deep as false nines, creating overloads in midfield, or Gonçalo Ramos offered a more traditional focal point when physical presence was required inside the penalty area.

A crucial element of Luis Enrique's tactical blueprint was the asymmetric role of the full-backs. Achraf Hakimi practically functioned as an auxiliary right-winger, stretching the pitch and providing a constant overlapping threat. On the opposite side, Nuno Mendes offered explosive underlaps or maintained a deeper position to form a back three during the build-up phase, ensuring defensive stability against swift counter-attacks. This structural fluidity made PSG nearly impossible to press effectively.

While the system was demanding and occasionally left space behind the high defensive line, the collective work rate and sheer dominance of possession mitigated those risks. The team defended aggressively from the front, suffocating opponents before they could even cross the halfway line. This tactical masterpiece culminated in the ultimate reward: a historic continental treble, cementing this iteration of PSG as one of the most tactically astute and dominant sides in modern European football.
